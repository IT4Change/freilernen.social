The basic principles of the Menschheitsfamilie Werkstatt e.v. apply

(1) Basic principles of personal responsibility

Each and every one of us is responsible for ourselves and our actions.
Each and every one of us focuses our attention on behaving as empathetically as possible.
Each and every one of us bases our actions and behavior on the golden rule:
“Do unto others as you would have them do unto you” and its reversal:
"Treat others as you would like to be treated yourself!"

(2) Basic principles of coexistence

The personal responsibility of each and every one of us gives rise to the following principles for
constructive and appreciative coexistence, which always remains focused on the topic at hand. Our coexistence is based on respectful and balanced interaction within the community.
We strive to reflect on and dissolve our conditioning on an equal footing.
To this end, we use communication methods such as theme-centered interaction (TCI), nonviolent communication, and active listening.
Our cooperation is characterized by joint decisions.
Our cooperation pursues goal-oriented action.
In the event of conflicts, we strive to find a solution in an objective and clear manner.
As the human family, we address issues in a way that is oriented toward the common good and solutions, open-ended,
spacious, objective, and conducive to growth. Disregard for these principles will be addressed and will have consequences.
These basic principles will remain in effect until they are replaced by a newer version.
